## Technical Notes and Compliance Information

**Aluminium Size Restriction**  
Per IEC 60228 Clause 5.2.1 b, stranded aluminium or aluminium alloy conductors shall have a nominal cross sectional area of not less than 10 mm^2.

**Reference Temperature**  
All resistance values are maximum values defined and tested at a base temperature of 20°C.

**Plain vs Metal Coated**  
Plain copper values are listed above. Metal coated or tinned wires generally exhibit higher resistance.

**Field Measurement Correction**  
To correct d.c. resistance measured at temperature t to the standard 20°C, the formula from Annex A applies:

R20 = Rt × kt

Where  
kt is the temperature correction factor  
L is the length of the cable in metres

**Aluminium Alloy Note**  
Resistance for aluminium alloy conductors should be agreed between manufacturer and purchaser. Solid alloy conductors of the same area typically have a resistance factor of 1.162 times higher than pure aluminium.
