# Maximum DC Conductor Resistance Table at 20°C and 90°C for Class 2 Stranded Conductors

## Scope

This table applies to Class 2 stranded conductors.

Resistance values are based on IEC 60228 Class 2 stranded conductor limits at 20°C. Values shown at 90°C are temperature adjusted reference values for DC resistance only.

Conductor diameter values are shown only where directly verified from manufacturer or utility documentation. Where no verified diameter was available, the field is left blank.

Sectorial, shaped, segmented and Milliken constructions are not interchangeable with ordinary circular compacted assumptions.

## Basis

Copper factor from 20°C to 90°C: 1.2751

Aluminium factor from 20°C to 90°C: 1.2821

Copper at 90°C = Copper at 20°C × 1.2751

Aluminium at 90°C = Aluminium at 20°C × 1.2821

## Technical Notes

Resistance values at 20°C are IEC 60228 Class 2 stranded conductor maximum DC resistance values.

Values at 90°C are calculated DC reference values only. They do not represent full AC operating behaviour.

Verified diameter values are included only where directly stated in manufacturer or utility documentation.

For smaller sizes, no verified bare conductor diameter has been inserted unless directly supported by manufacturer data. IEC 60228 provides resistance limits and dimensional guidance, but not universal actual manufacturer conductor diameters for those smaller Class 2 sizes.

For larger sizes, especially 1200 mm² and above, IEC 60228 explicitly allows Class 2 constructions to be made from 4, 5 or 6 equal segments, commonly referred to as Milliken construction. These are not ordinary simple circular conductors and must be treated as manufacturer specific designs.

Large HV aluminium conductors at 1200 mm² and 1600 mm² in the uploaded schedules are explicitly stated as Milliken compacted constructions.

Conductor diameter must not be inferred from overall cable diameter.

Overall cable diameter and bare conductor diameter are different parameters and must not be mixed.

## Terminations and Installation Risk

Termination selection must be based on the exact conductor construction, actual verified conductor diameter and approved accessory range.

Aluminium conductors require appropriate bimetallic terminations and installation controls.

Milliken, segmented, sectorial and other shaped conductors require specialist accessory selection and manufacturer supported termination design.

Crimping aluminium and shaped conductors carries elevated installation risk.

## Disclaimer

This table is provided for general technical reference only.

The resistance values shown are DC conductor resistance values only. They do not represent AC resistance, reactance, impedance or full system behaviour.

All values must be independently checked against the relevant standard, the exact cable manufacturer data, accessory manufacturer data, applicable local and international standards and a suitably qualified professional engineer.

Where required by project risk, contract, insurer, client or law, manufacturer confirmation and sign off by a suitably qualified Chartered Electrical Engineer should be obtained and retained with project records.
