# Maximum DC Conductor Resistance Table at 20°C and 90°C (Class 2 Stranded Conductors)

## Scope

This table applies to stranded conductors (Class 2), including aluminium, primarily for circular compacted constructions.

Solid conductors (Class 1) have higher permissible resistance and must be verified separately against IEC 60228 Table 1.

Sectorial, Milliken or other non-circular conductor geometries are not directly represented unless explicitly stated.

---

## Basis

20°C values align with IEC 60228 limits.

Values at 90°C are derived from 20°C resistance and are consistent with manufacturer voltage drop data and utility specifications at operating temperature.

The values presented represent DC conductor resistance only and do not include AC effects such as skin effect or proximity effect.

## Technical Notes

- Aluminium conductors minimum size: 10 mm²  
- Base reference temperature: 20°C  
- 90°C values represent conductor resistance at operating temperature  
- Resistance at operating temperature is reflected in manufacturer voltage drop data  
- The values represent DC resistance only  

---

## Conductor Construction and Geometry

- Conductor diameters must always be verified against manufacturer datasheets  
- Circular compacted assumptions apply only to smaller sizes  

- At larger sizes, typically above 1000 mm², conductors transition to **Milliken (segmented) construction**

- Milliken conductors:
  - consist of multiple insulated segments
  - are not solid circular conductors
  - have different electrical and thermal behaviour
  - introduce additional interface and AC effects

- Conductor diameter scaling is therefore not linear and must not be extrapolated

---

## Terminations and Installation Risk

- Always confirm compatibility of lugs, glands and connectors with cable and accessory manufacturers  

- Aluminium conductors require bimetallic terminations  

- Shear bolt connectors must be:
  - type tested  
  - suitable for conductor construction  
  - compliant with DNO specifications  

- Crimping aluminium conductors carries elevated risk due to:
  - oxide formation  
  - incorrect tooling  
  - poor compression control  

- Milliken and segmented conductors require specialist termination design and validation

## Disclaimer

The values presented in this table are DC conductor resistance values only and are provided for general technical reference.

They do not represent AC resistance, reactance, impedance or full system electrical behaviour.

AC electrical parameters must be determined using appropriate calculation tools, manufacturer data and project specific installation conditions.

All values must be independently verified against applicable standards, manufacturer data and by appropriately qualified professionals prior to use.

Manufacturer confirmation and sign off by a suitably qualified Chartered Electrical Engineer may be required. Records of calculations, approvals and professional indemnity insurance should be maintained.
