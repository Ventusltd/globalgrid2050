## Summary of Energy Conversion and System Insight

The UK consumes approximately 141 Mtoe of primary energy annually, equivalent to around 1644 TWh when converted using a factor of 11.63 TWh per Mtoe.

This table represents primary energy use across sectors, not final useful energy. A key insight is that most fossil fuel energy is lost during conversion. Internal combustion vehicles lose around 70 to 75 percent of energy as heat, while thermal power stations lose 50 to 65 percent.

As a result, the total primary energy figure significantly overstates the energy actually required to deliver services such as transport, heating, and industrial work.

## Logic Used in This Table

1. Raw data sourced from ONS in Mtoe by sector, activity, and fuel  
2. Conversion applied:  
   TWh = Mtoe × 11.63  
3. Values rounded to nearest whole TWh for clarity  
4. Percentage calculated relative to total UK primary energy  
5. Ranked by descending Mtoe contribution  

## System-Level Interpretation

Electrification changes the system fundamentally:

- Electric vehicles convert around 70 percent of energy into motion  
- Heat pumps deliver 3 to 5 units of heat per unit of electricity  
- Electric motors are significantly more efficient than combustion systems  

Because of this, a fully electrified UK would likely require only 500 to 700 TWh of electricity rather than replacing the full 1644 TWh of primary energy one for one.

This means the transition is not about replacing fossil energy volume, but about eliminating conversion losses and delivering the same output with far less input energy.
