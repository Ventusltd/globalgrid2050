DNV indicates that **global solar PV capacity will exceed 3000 gigawatt by the end of 2025**, confirming the three terawatt peak milestone.

## Executive Summary

Global solar deployment reached unprecedented scale in 2025 with **650 gigawatt of new capacity installed worldwide** according to industry outlooks including SolarPower Europe and BloombergNEF. China dominated expansion, installing **315 gigawatt in a single year**, accounting for nearly half of global additions. Ember analysis shows **380 gigawatt of solar capacity installed during the first half of 2025**, confirming the rapid acceleration of global deployment. Cumulative installed solar capacity worldwide therefore reaches **3000 gigawatt**. Looking ahead, research from the Terawatt Workshop organised by Fraunhofer ISE with participation from NREL indicates that **75 terawatt of globally deployed solar PV will be required by 2050** to support deep electrification and decarbonisation of the global energy system.
