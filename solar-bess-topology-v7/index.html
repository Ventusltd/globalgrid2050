Solar Engineering Functions Supported By V7

V7 is a testing workspace for early solar PV and BESS engineering review. It helps users expose assumptions before projects move into detailed design, procurement, EPC delivery or investment approval.

1. Site and grid screening

Assess where a solar or BESS project sits in relation to grid infrastructure, substations and likely connection logic.

2. Solar BESS topology review

Review the relationship between solar PV, BESS, inverters, transformers and the grid connection point.

3. Single line diagram thinking

Visualise early SLD logic across DC, AC, LV, MV and grid interface assumptions.

4. DC string level reasoning

Support early review of DC string voltage margin, inverter input assumptions and DC side configuration.

5. AC and LV interface review

Support review of LV AC assumptions, inverter feeder status, insulation basis and transformer connection logic.

6. Cable route assumption review

Study route assumptions before formal design and before procurement decisions harden.

7. Cable geometry visualisation

Inspect cable arrangement, grouping, routing, trench logic and physical layout behaviour.

8. Cable loss awareness

Expose how cable distance, arrangement and topology may affect electrical losses and project value.

9. Thermal assumption visibility

Flag whether thermal assumptions are complete, missing, uncertain or requiring formal review.

10. Protection assumption visibility

Flag whether protection assumptions are complete, incomplete or need engineering review.

11. Manufacturer data confirmation

Track whether cable, containment, inverter or equipment data has been confirmed.

12. Module layout review

Review physical PV module count, rating, footprint, row, pitch and layout assumptions.

13. Engineering risk status

Mark assumptions as status unknown, study required, review required or formal design required.

14. Commercial engineering linkage

Connect topology, cable distance, grid proximity and losses to CAPEX, revenue, profit, cashflow and IRR sensitivity.
